# General Recommendations for AI Prompts

Guide of best practices for writing effective prompts designed to guide developers or generate quality code.

## Fundamental Principles

1. **Clarity and Specificity**
    - Be direct and avoid ambiguities
    - Define exactly what you expect as a result
    - Use precise and appropriate technical language

2. **Logical Structure**
    - Organize instructions hierarchically
    - Start with the overall objective, then specific details
    - Use numbered or bulleted lists for better readability

3. **Relevant Context**
    - Provide necessary information about the project, technologies, and constraints
    - Mention patterns, conventions, or frameworks in use
    - Indicate the target audience (junior developers, senior, DevOps, etc.)

4. **Format and Examples**
    - Define the expected response format (code, documentation, step-by-step, etc.)
    - Include examples when appropriate to illustrate expectations
    - Specify relevant programming languages or technologies

5. **Traceability and Documentation**
    - Keep a record of important instructions
    - Document decisions and reasoning behind prompts
    - Update instructions as the project evolves

## Essential Elements of a Good Prompt

- **Clear objective**: What needs to be done?
- **Context**: What is the environment and what are the limitations?
- **Success criteria**: How will you know it was done correctly?
- **Constraints**: What should not be done?
- **Output format**: How should the response be structured?

## Best Practices

- Review and refine prompts based on feedback
- Be consistent in nomenclature and terminology
- Adapt the level of detail to the target audience
- Provide examples of expected inputs and outputs when complex
- Document assumptions and external dependencies
- REQUIRED: Always ask for permission before undertaking any task.
